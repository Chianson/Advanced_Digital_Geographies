Seattle and King County Washington food banks.

Get free food, groceries, meals and other forms of assistance from non-profits or charities in Seattle and King County Washington. There are dozens of soup kitchens, food banks and pantries. The centers are located throughout the region and offer a variety of resources and services to the needy in the community.

In addition to offering basic needs such as free food, groceries, commodities, and other nutritional aids, a number of additional assistance programs are also provided. For example some centers provide clothing, diapers, and even furniture. A limited number of locations in Seattle and King County can also offer direct financial assistance to pay bills, rent, and other emergency expenses. Or the case managers at a pantry may provide referrals for those resources.

As indicated, a food pantry or center is also a great place to call or stop by for information and referrals to local charities, non-profits, and learn about both state of Washington and federal government assistance. The sites and the staff that work at each center are committed to assisting the less fortunate. Many food banks can also help people apply for government assistance such as TANF, SNAP food stamps, welfare, or other aid.
